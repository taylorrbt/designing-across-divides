CALLS

Diptych Banner Designs

Deadline: November 16th, 2018

Goal: Graphic Designers are invited to design a pair of banners that illustrate how to bridge or address a divide in a format that would be positioned to the left and right of a light pole.

Disrespectful content will not be chosen. (We know, we get as worked up as you do). As you research some very powerful poster designers, think of which could be viewed by both sides without feeling shamed. Humor, irony, paradox, sense of loss, opportunities for empathy, common ground or change of lens can be ways to approach a new look at an old problem. A Smile in the Mind by Beryl McAlhone shows 28 types of wit in visual approaches. For considering the problem of societal divides in relation to freedom of speech, my class will be reading All Minus One, Jonathan Haidt’s, new book of John Stuart Mill essays from 1859 beautifully illustrated by Dave Cicirelli. Haidt has several great TED Talks that are also good background, as are Sarah Kohn’s. Files should be submitted to the Designingacrossdivides.org web site by 11:59pm November 16. The submission tab is not up yet. Contact Eve Faulkes with questions. efaulkes@mail.wvu.edu

Lighting Talk Showcases

Lightning talks of 5 minutes will showcase projects that have done a great job integrating design with dialogue and deliberation in advancing community change across social, political, economic, and/or environmental divides.
